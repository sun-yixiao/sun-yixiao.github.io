Localization and Globalization:
Advertisements for Imported Commodities in Modern China 

2022.06 - Currently
Leader of Project

After the Western powers opened the gates of China in modern times, capitalist culture entered China along with commodities and media. China's traditionally home-based society was affected by the impact of democracy and science, which led to the emergence of a variety of theories and trends of thought. As a medium, advertising was both a witness to the current situation and a natural carrier for the dissemination of ideas. The use of localized and globalized advertising strategies was a reflection of the social value tendencies of the time. This study takes foreign advertisements in Yuehua Newspaper and Hankow Chinese and Western Newspaper published between 1932 and 1937 in Guangzhou and Hankow, the two major ports of commerce in modern China, as the object of study, and analyzes the localization and globalization narratives of advertisements by manual coding and logistic regression methods. The results confirm that there is a tension between localization and globalization in Republican advertisements. This study differs from the previous qualitative approach based on case studies and innovatively uses an empirical-quantitative approach to study the history of advertising in the Republic of China. At the same time, the study combines the viewpoint of cultural imperialism with the study of advertising history, and views modern Chinese foreign advertising as a way for capitalist consumer culture to settle in, dismantle the inherent local culture and try to pull China into the capitalist world, providing a small glimpse into the era of Republican society. Even in the smallest aspects of people's food, clothing, and housing, localization and globalization still compete fiercely.

Traversing the Valley of Despair:
Online Donation on Weibo During COVID-19 Pandemic

2022.02 - Currently
Participant

Facing severe public crises such as wars, natural disasters and epidemics, it is inevitable that people will fall into the valley of despair. Although social media can facilitate crowdfunding and online donations, it also suffers from the problem of emotional overload. Drawing on the social mobilization theory, this study aims to investigate how to effectively mobilize the public to take action and traverse the valley of despair during public health emergencies. Using a large-scale dataset of Sina Weibo, we build machine learning models to classify the categories of posts and conduct panel data analysis to test our hypotheses. The findings suggest that: first, the valley of despair indeed exists during the outbreak of Covid-19 on social media; second, issue engagement plays a crucial role in promoting online donation; third, the higher the user threshold, the more difficult it is for users to be activated to donate; finally, emotion mediates the influence of issue engagement on online donation. In all, this study contributes to our knowledge by underlining both the strength and weaknesses of online donation. The limitations and implications for future research are discussed.
